About Me

Ph.D. candidate, University of Wisconsin–Madison

Brandon Corder is a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Botany at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where his research explores the evolution of plant-fungal symbioses and trophic strategies in orchids. Combining stable isotope analysis, high-throughput sequencing, and plastome assembly, Brandon investigates how ecological and phylogenetic patterns shape mycoheterotrophy in diverse orchid lineages. He is passionate about science communication, field botany, and making the natural world more accessible to all.
