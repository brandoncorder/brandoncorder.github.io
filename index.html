About Me

Ph.D. candidate, University of Wisconsin–Madison

I'm a Botany PhD at the University of Wisconsin–Madison studying the evolution of plant heterotrophy with a focus on the fungal associations and ecology of North American orchids, the tribe Triphoreae, and the subfamily Vanilloideae. In my research, I combine stable isotope analyses, fungal metabarcoding and high-throughput sequencing, extensive fieldwork, and natural history collections research to investigate how ecological and phylogenetic patterns shape the evolution of heterotrophy.
